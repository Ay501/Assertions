POULTRY SECTION

This is the scetion that deals with the general state and wellbeing of terrestial birds.Birds can majorly be divided into the following,

1.Layers

2.Broilers

3.Turkeys

4.Cockrels etc

LAYERS

These are known as lying birds. They are breeded for the main purpose of of producing eggs. They have high resistance to diseses and are very easy to raise. The fact that they have high resistnce to diseases does not mean these birds are immune to diseases. These birds are highly profittable in the sense that at old age, they can be sold way more than the amount used in buying them.

These eggs are sold in either paper or plastic crates

NOTE- Layers can lay for more than a year and six months and are not so big in size.
Water (H2O) is very vital to their growth at 30oC They are often referred to as NATIVE FOWL

Also in should be emphatically noted that layers are very sensitive to the feeding system adopted by the livestock farmer. If there is an change in the brand of feed at any time, it will affect the laying capacity of the birds. Also, if there is a change in the time of feeding, it will still affect their laying capacity

BROILERS

These are the set of birds breeded for the main purpose of producing meeat. They are breeded for a span of four to five months as the case may be. Mind you, they can also be raised for a longer life span

They are mostly white in colour. If these set of birds are grown for some time, they become what we refer to as PARENT COCK AND HENS respectively

TURKEYS

These are also grown or breeded for meat production but these class are relatively bigger than the other birds. They are very expensive to raise and are also very expensive to sell after breeding life

Turkeys can be divided into two;

1. Agric Turkeys

2. Native Turkeys

COCKRELS

These are the smallest of them but also the strongest of them all. They are very stong and can survive anywhere
